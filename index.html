Chapter 01

BMI & Weight: Who's to Blame
When the Numbers Go Up?

Body Mass Index is a key indicator of physical health. But why can one student eat fast food and stay lean, while another gains weight despite trying hard?

🧬

The Role of Genetics

Science proves we inherit our metabolic rate and predisposition to obesity. The FTO gene is a well-documented example. If a teenager's parents struggle with weight, their risk increases by 40–70%. Hereditary endocrine conditions also play a significant role — these factors are largely outside your control.

🏃‍♂️

The Role of Lifestyle

However, genetics is only the foundation. A sedentary lifestyle (less than 60 minutes of physical activity a day) and a diet high in sugar and refined carbohydrates can undermine even the best genetic starting point. Choices made daily accumulate into long-term outcomes.

Chapter 02

The Fatigue Epidemic:
Why Are High Schoolers Always Tired?

Between ages 15 and 18, teenagers face massive stress: exams, social media, hormonal changes. But why is everyone constantly exhausted?

🧬

Genetic Factors

Genetic chronotypes are real. Some of us are biologically wired as "night owls" — our brains release melatonin much later at night. Chronic fatigue can also link to inherited anemia (iron deficiency) or thyroid issues that affect energy metabolism.

The Sleep Gap

✅

⚠️
Actual average: 6–7 hours

📱

Lifestyle & Sleep Habits

Poor lifestyle choices are the primary cause of chronic fatigue for 90% of students. The culprits:

Screen Time Before Bed

Blue light from smartphones suppresses melatonin production, making it harder to fall asleep and reducing sleep quality.

Energy Drinks & Caffeine

Overconsumption of caffeine creates a cycle: fatigue → stimulant → poor sleep → more fatigue. Common in exam periods.

Weekend Oversleeping

Sleeping in on weekends disrupts the circadian rhythm — the biological clock resets, making Monday mornings brutal.

High Stress Levels

Cortisol (the stress hormone) interferes with deep sleep stages. Academic pressure, social anxiety, and family stress all contribute.

Chapter 04

Hack Your Genetics:
An Action Plan for High Schoolers

Our research clearly shows: genetics gives us our starting conditions, but lifestyle determines the final result. You can't change your DNA — but you CAN change your habits.

01

🛌 Hack Your Sleep

  • Put your phone away 45 minutes before bed
  • Sleep in a cool, dark room (around 18°C)
  • Same bedtime every day — including weekends
  • No energy drinks after 3pm
  • Try a 20-minute power nap instead of 3 hours
02

🥗 Nutritional Balance

  • Don't blame "heavy bones" — track what you eat for 3 days
  • Focus on protein and fiber at every meal
  • Drink water instead of sugary sodas and juices
  • Eat breakfast — it regulates hunger hormones all day
  • Your genes don't force you to buy chips!
03

🏃‍♀️ Smart Movement

  • Hate running? Dance, skateboard, or play volleyball
  • Any physical activity reduces cortisol (stress) levels
  • 60 min/day is the target — break it into 3×20 min
  • Walk during phone calls or while listening to music
  • Aim for consistency over intensity
🎯

The 2-Week Challenge

Try just ONE habit from each category for 14 days. Research shows it takes 2 weeks to notice measurable changes in energy levels and 6–8 weeks for stable BMI shifts. Small consistent steps beat dramatic short-term efforts every time.

Final Verdict

The Answer to Our
Research Question

After examining the science, surveying our peers, and analyzing the data — here is what we found.

Lifestyle Choices

Chronic fatigue and unhealthy BMI in modern teenagers are rarely caused by inherited diseases alone. In the vast majority of cases, they result from sleep deprivation, poor diets, and a lack of physical activity. Genetics provides the starting conditions — but we are the architects of our own bodies.
